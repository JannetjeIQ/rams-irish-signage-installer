# Legal Context: Republic of Ireland

The legal and regulatory frame the specialist works within. Read this once. The specialist references it implicitly in every RAMS.

## Primary statutes binding any contractor on an Irish site

- **Safety, Health and Welfare at Work Act 2005.** The overarching duty. Section 8 requires the employer to provide a safe system of work. Section 19 requires hazard identification and risk assessment. Section 20 requires a Safety Statement.
- **S.I. No. 299 of 2007: Safety, Health and Welfare at Work (General Application) Regulations.** Covers PPE, manual handling, work equipment, display screen equipment, first aid, signage. Applies to every job, construction site or not.
- **S.I. No. 291 of 2013: Safety, Health and Welfare at Work (Construction) Regulations.** Applies whenever the work is classified as "construction". Most fit-out and signage installation work falls under this. Introduces PSCS (Project Supervisor Construction Stage), PSDP (Project Supervisor Design Process), Safe Pass requirement, and duties on every contractor on site.
- **S.I. No. 445 of 2012: Chemical Agents Regulations.** Ireland's COSHH equivalent. Applies to adhesives, cleaners, solvents.
- **Fire Services Acts 1981 and 2003 (as amended).** Triggered for any work that affects fire compartmentation, escape routes, or alarm systems (rare for signage but possible for hoardings or interior wayfinding inside emergency routes).

## Guidance documents the specialist relies on

- **HSA RAMS Guidance Document.** The official Irish guidance. Defines the 8-section template structure used by this specialist.
- **HSA Interactive RAMS Template.** Free, downloadable from hsa.ie. Compatible with the structure used here.
- **HSA Code of Practice for Access and Working Scaffolds.**
- **HSA guidance on Mobile Elevated Work Platforms.** Used for any RAMS involving a MEWP.
- **Construction Safety Partnership Advisory Committee outputs.**

## When a RAMS is required

Produce a RAMS for any job where one or more is true:

- Work takes place on an active construction site or commercial fit-out under S.I. 291/2013.
- Work involves working at height (over 2m without collective fall protection, or any ladder work).
- The client, main contractor, PSCS, or building manager has requested one.
- The work involves public exposure (live retail, hospitality, transport, event venue, healthcare).
- Out-of-hours, overnight, or lone working.
- Use of MEWPs, mobile towers, or scaffolding.
- The work requires a permit-to-work (hot work, work at height, confined space).

For a small signage replacement in a private office with no height, public, or construction trigger, a simplified risk assessment without a full Method Statement may be sufficient. When in doubt, produce a full RAMS.

## Roles on an Irish construction site

- **PSCS: Project Supervisor Construction Stage.** Appointed by the client on construction sites. Coordinates safety across all contractors. Receives every contractor's RAMS before works begin and may require amendments.
- **PSDP: Project Supervisor Design Process.** Appointed for design-stage safety coordination. Rarely interacts with the install contractor directly.
- **Main Contractor / Principal Contractor.** Frequently the same as the PSCS on commercial fit-outs. Issues induction, welfare, permits.
- **Client.** Pays for the work. May also be the premises occupier (retail, hospitality, healthcare).

## When the contractor is itself the PSCS

Rare for signage-only work but possible on standalone signage packages commissioned directly. If the contractor is PSCS:

- A Construction Phase Plan is required before work begins.
- If the project exceeds 500 person-days, or 30 working days with more than 20 workers simultaneously, or involves demolition, it is **notifiable** to HSA via Form AF1.
- The contractor coordinates any other contractors on the same work.

This scenario should be flagged before accepting the work.

## When the client imposes their own RAMS template

Some main contractors (especially in retail estates, healthcare, transport, government estate) require submission on their own RAMS template instead of the contractor's standard. Approach:

1. Accept the imposed template as the format.
2. Populate it with the same content the specialist would produce on the standard 8-section structure.
3. Preserve the contractor's standing rules (verbatim trading name, retractable blades, cut-resistant gloves, RECI exclusion) inside the imposed template.
4. Attach the contractor's insurance certificate, Safety Statement, and competency certificates as appendices regardless of template.

## Geographic scope of this specialist

- **Republic of Ireland.** Default scope. HSA framework as above.
- **Northern Ireland.** HSENI plus UK CDM Regulations 2015. Equivalent duties, different citation. The specialist will refuse to draft for NI work without an updated context file.
- **Great Britain (occasional).** HSE plus CDM 2015. Same applies as NI.

To extend the specialist for NI / GB, duplicate this file and substitute HSA references with HSE / CDM 2015. Risk matrix, install steps, and competency framework are largely portable; insurance height limits and Safe Pass / CSCS substitutions need attention.

## Disclaimer

This specialist is a drafting aid, not a substitute for competent H&S advice. A RAMS produced here must be reviewed and signed off by a competent person in the contractor's organisation before issue to a client or PSCS. For complex sites (high-risk demolition, asbestos, confined space, work over public roads), engage a chartered safety consultant.
