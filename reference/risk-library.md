# Risk Library: Signage Installation

Common hazards on Irish signage installation jobs, with suggested initial scoring and controls. Seed the Risk Prioritisation Table from this library; tune for the actual site.

Initial scoring assumes no controls applied. Residual scoring is calculated after the listed controls.

---

## Work at height

- **Activity:** Installing signage above shoulder height; ladder, step ladder, mobile tower, MEWP, or scaffold work.
- **Initial:** Likelihood 4 (Likely), Severity 4 (Major), Rating 16, Band Very High
- **Controls:**
  - Right access equipment for the height and duration: MEWP or tower for any sustained work above 2m, ladder only for short reach.
  - Equipment inspected before use, tag in date.
  - PASMA-certified assembly for towers; IPAF-certified operation for MEWPs.
  - No overreaching from a ladder; reposition.
  - Harness with attachment point if working at height without collective protection.
  - Two-person rule when ladder is used on uneven ground or in public space.
- **Residual:** L 2, S 3, R 6, Band Medium

---

## Manual handling

- **Activity:** Lifting and positioning panels, glass, large prints, fascia sections, totems, pre-fabricated lightbox shells.
- **Initial:** L 4, S 3, R 12, Band High
- **Controls:**
  - Two-fitter lift for anything awkward, heavy, or bulky (default: any panel over 1m or any glass).
  - Mechanical aids: suction cups for glass, trolleys for large panels, panel lifters where available.
  - Kinetic lifting technique, no twisting under load.
  - Clear route planned and walked before the lift.
- **Residual:** L 2, S 2, R 4, Band Low

---

## Cuts from blades and sharp edges

- **Activity:** Trimming vinyl and laminate; handling cut aluminium, ACM, dibond, or acrylic edges.
- **Initial:** L 4, S 2, R 8, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Retractable blades only, blade retracted when not cutting.
  - Cut-resistant gloves for all cutting and for handling cut edges.
  - Cut away from the body.
  - Used blades disposed of in sharps container or sealed rigid pack.
- **Residual:** L 1, S 2, R 2, Band Low

---

## Electrical (existing services, power tools)

- **Activity:** Drilling into walls near services, use of corded or cordless power tools.
- **Initial:** L 2, S 4, R 8, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Confirm no services in drilling zone with site manager; use cable / pipe detector before drilling.
  - Inspect power tools and leads before use; PAT in date.
  - RCD on mains tools.
  - Live electrical isolation, disconnect, and reconnect of mains-powered signage handled by a third-party RECI registered electrician (always; this is excluded from the contractor's scope).
- **Residual:** L 1, S 3, R 3, Band Low

---

## Slips, trips, and falls at ground level

- **Activity:** Moving tools, packaging, offcuts around the work area.
- **Initial:** L 3, S 2, R 6, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Clean as you go; offcuts and packaging bagged immediately.
  - Cables routed along walls, not across walkways; cable covers in public areas.
  - Spill kit and cloths available for adhesive or cleaner spills.
- **Residual:** L 2, S 2, R 4, Band Low

---

## Public interaction (live retail, hospitality, healthcare, public space)

- **Activity:** Installing in an operational site with customers, staff, patients, or pedestrians present.
- **Initial:** L 4, S 4, R 16, Band Very High
- **Controls:**
  - Cordon the work zone with rigid barriers (A-frames, bollards), not tape alone in high-traffic areas.
  - Warning signage: "Work in progress", "Caution: overhead work".
  - Fitter pauses work when public is within the hazard zone.
  - Out-of-hours or pre-opening work where daytime exclusion is impractical (agree with site manager).
  - On healthcare sites, additional consideration for vulnerable users (slow movement, wheelchair access, sensory impairments).
- **Residual:** L 2, S 3, R 6, Band Medium

---

## Dust and debris

- **Activity:** Drilling, cutting, sanding, grinding.
- **Initial:** L 4, S 2, R 8, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Vacuum extraction on power tools where feasible.
  - Dust sheets to protect finished floors and stock.
  - Dust mask (FFP2 minimum) where extraction is not feasible.
- **Residual:** L 2, S 1, R 2, Band Low

---

## Noise

- **Activity:** Drilling, impact driving, cutting.
- **Initial:** L 3, S 2, R 6, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Hearing protection for fitter during sustained power tool use.
  - Respect site noise windows (especially retail, residential adjacent, healthcare).
- **Residual:** L 2, S 1, R 2, Band Low

---

## Vehicle movement (loading bay, car park, service yard)

- **Activity:** Loading and unloading vans, crossing car parks with stock.
- **Initial:** L 3, S 4, R 12, Band High
- **Controls:**
  - Hi-vis worn from van door onwards.
  - Designated loading bay where available, agreed with site manager.
  - No reversing without a banksman in busy bays.
  - Awareness of pedestrian flow at site entrance.
- **Residual:** L 2, S 3, R 6, Band Medium

---

## Adhesive fumes and solvents

- **Activity:** Applying adhesives, cleaning substrate with IPA or other solvents.
- **Initial:** L 3, S 2, R 6, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Ventilate the work area; open doors and windows where possible.
  - Respirator (half-mask A1P2 or A2P2) for prolonged solvent use in enclosed areas.
  - Follow the SDS for the specific product (Appendix C of the RAMS).
- **Residual:** L 2, S 1, R 2, Band Low

---

## Lone working

- **Activity:** Single-fitter install, especially after hours or in isolated premises.
- **Initial:** L 2, S 4, R 8, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Fitter checks in with the project manager on arrival and departure.
  - Mobile phone charged; site contact number held; ICE contact accessible.
  - No lone working at height above 3m.
  - Reschedule if conditions on arrival are unsafe for solo work.
- **Residual:** L 1, S 3, R 3, Band Low

---

## Working in occupied premises out of hours

- **Activity:** Night installs, early-morning installs, weekend installs.
- **Initial:** L 3, S 3, R 9, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Agreed access times with site manager and security.
  - Adequate task lighting if site lighting is off.
  - Check-in and check-out logged with security.
  - Tiredness management: scheduled breaks, no extended unbroken night shifts.
- **Residual:** L 2, S 2, R 4, Band Low

---

## Weather (external installs)

- **Activity:** External fascia, monolith, totem, hoarding work.
- **Initial:** L 3, S 3, R 9, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Wind limit: do not lift large panels in winds over 23 mph (10 m/s); MEWP wind limits per manufacturer (typically 12.5 m/s for boom MEWPs).
  - No work at height in lightning, heavy rain affecting visibility, or snow.
  - Reschedule rather than push.
  - Substrate adhesion: many adhesives have a minimum application temperature (typically 10 degrees C); check SDS in cold weather.
- **Residual:** L 2, S 2, R 4, Band Low

---

## Fragile or delicate site finishes

- **Activity:** Working in a recently completed fit-out (polished concrete floors, fresh plaster, bespoke joinery, glazed shopfronts).
- **Initial:** L 3, S 2, R 6, Band Medium
- **Controls:**
  - Floor protection (Correx, dust sheets, foam).
  - No dragging of equipment.
  - Photograph the area before starting; note any pre-existing damage to site contact.
  - Soft mallet rather than hammer where finish proximity is a concern.
- **Residual:** L 1, S 2, R 2, Band Low

---

## Working near or above the public on a footpath or roadway

- **Activity:** External fascia or signage above a public footpath or live roadway.
- **Initial:** L 4, S 5, R 20, Band Very High
- **Controls:**
  - Footpath licence from the local authority where required (Section 254, Planning and Development Act 2000, in ROI).
  - Banksman or pedestrian marshall.
  - Solid hoarding or pedestrian deflection where overhead risk cannot be eliminated.
  - Out-of-hours work where the footpath cannot be safely managed in daylight.
  - No tools or fixings carried up or down without a tether or bag.
- **Residual:** L 2, S 4, R 8, Band Medium

---

## Notes for use

This library is curated to signage installation in Ireland. It is not exhaustive. For any hazard not covered (e.g., asbestos in a pre-1990 building, work in a confined space, work near overhead power lines), engage a chartered safety consultant and do not rely solely on this specialist.

When seeding the matrix, default to:

1. Work at height (almost every job)
2. Manual handling (almost every job)
3. Cuts (almost every job)
4. The site-context hazard (one of: public interaction, weather, fragile finishes, vehicle movement)
5. Any specific hazard the survey or the client flagged
